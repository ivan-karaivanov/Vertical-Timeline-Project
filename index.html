Development Timeline

A timeline of Important events for JavaScript.

  • 1995 JavaScript is created by Brendan Eich in just 10 days while working at Netscape Communications. It was originally called "Mocha" and later "LiveScript" before settling on the name JavaScript.
  • 1996 Netscape submits JavaScript to the ECMA International standards organization, leading to the first standardized version called ECMAScript, which helps ensure cross-browser compatibility.
  • 1999 ECMAScript 3 is published, becoming the widely adopted version of JavaScript and forming the basis for most modern web development.
  • 2005 Jesse James Garrett coins the term "Ajax" (Asynchronous JavaScript and XML), highlighting the use of JavaScript for dynamic web applications.
  • 2008 ECMAScript 4, a major update, is abandoned due to disagreements among stakeholders. However, this leads to the development of ECMAScript 5. The following year ECMAScript 5 is released, bringing significant improvements, including strict mode and new array methods.
  • 2015 ECMAScript 6 (ES6), also known as ECMAScript 2015, is finalized, introducing major enhancements like classes, arrow functions, and modules.
